Is Lausanne's housing stock evolving to match its changing social fabric?

Nearly half of Lausanne's residents are foreign-born, and almost one in two households now consists of a single person. Yet the city's housing stock has barely adapted in the past 15 years — raising the question of whether supply still matches demand.

Who lives in Lausanne?

Lausanne's population has grown and transformed since 1979 — absorbing waves of international residents, shifting younger, and spreading unevenly across its 18 districts. This lens traces that demographic transformation and establishes the social context in which housing demand emerges.

Lens 2

How do people live and work?

Single-person households have surged while average household size shrinks. Women hold a majority of jobs in Lausanne — yet a persistent part-time gap means their full-time equivalent share remains lower. This shift toward smaller households directly increases demand for small apartments — the key question Lens 3 will answer.

What does housing offer?

Lausanne's housing stock grew 12% between 2010 and 2024 — but its distribution across room sizes barely shifted. This lens compares supply to demand: are small apartments multiplying fast enough to serve a city that is overwhelmingly made up of singles and couples?
